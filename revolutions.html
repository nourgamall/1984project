Prevalence of Revolutions

Finally, Orwell believed that it is near impossible to revolt against a totalitarian regime. However, there are several instances in history that prove otherwise. One of these instances is the revolutions of 1989 and many Eastern European countries were able to successfully gain their independence. Another example is the Cuban revolution where the Cuban people were able to overthrow Fulgencio Batista, the country’s dictator.

“Even today, in a period of decline, the average human being is physically better off than he was a few centuries ago. But no advance in wealth, no softening of manners, no reform or revolution has ever brought human equality a millimetre nearer. From the point of view of the Low, no historic change has ever meant much more than a change in the name of their masters."
